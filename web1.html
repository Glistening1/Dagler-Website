Course Overview

This course teaches students to develop Web sites using Hypertext Markup Language version 5 (HTML5) and Cascading Style Sheets (CSS). They'll also insert images, create hyperlinks, and add tables, forms, videos and audio to Web pages, as well as using HTML5 Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) to extend the functionality of Web pages. Other topics include validating HTML code, recognizing the importance of search engine optimization (SEO), and implementing fundamental design concepts. Additional topics include an intro to JavaScript, Image Maps, BootStrap, SVG, and PGP.

Grading Policies

Grades are based on the percent of total points earned from assignments, activities, and projects. Points are cumulative for each term. Here is the grading scale for out class:

Letter Percent Behavior
A 90%-100% A student who is excellent in computer science and an outstanding student.
B 80%-89.9% A student who is excellent in computer science and an average student, or average in computer science and a great student.
C 70%-79.9% A student who is average in computer science and an average student, or excellent in computer science and a poor student.
D/F 0%-69.9% A student who needs to retake the course because of poor study skills and lack of effort.

Students are expected to complete and submit their own work. Involvement in any activity for the purpose of copying, cheating or benefiting from work that is not 100% their own will be subject to consequences as outlined in the Academic Code of Condunct.
